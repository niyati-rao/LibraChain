LibraChain: Decentralized Library System

A centralized library system limits the number of books available for lending and doesnâ€™t promote peer-to-peer lending. The project, LibraChain, aims to decentralize libraries using Hyperledger Fabric. In traditional libraries, cataloging, lending, and tracking the usage of books and other materials can be complex and often rely on centralized systems which are less flexible and prone to attacks and being taken down. LibraChain seeks to bring transparency, accountability, and accessibility to library operations without a central authority such as a library through blockchain technology.

LibraChain has three primary participants: Administrator, libraries/individual lenders, and borrowers. Each book or material in the library will be represented as a unique digital asset on the blockchain. Users can borrow up to 3 books at a time, and each default is penalized by a reduction in the standard lending period (initially set to 7 days).

Key Features:
  1. Cataloging and Ownership Tracking: Every book or material added to the collection is recorded as a digital asset on the blockchain.
  2. Lending and Request Approval: Borrowers can borrow items from the library, and requests for lending are submitted through the blockchain. The lender/library reviews and approves lending requests digitally, ensuring efficient and accountable management of library materials.
  3. Immutable Records: All library transactions (additions, loans, and returns) are permanently recorded on the blockchain and can be viewed by the borrowers, libraries, and the administrator.
  4. Late Returns and Decrease in Lending Periods: Implementing Smart contracts to automate managing due dates, late returns, and associated decrease in lending periods and flagging users.

All the users of this application would interact with the Hyperledger through a website that would implement all the client-side functionalities.
